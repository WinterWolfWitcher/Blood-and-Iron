A Note on Arms & Heraldry

Military kit is largely shared across all seven kingdoms — gambeson base layer, chainmail coif at the neck and shoulders, plate at the legs, waist, gauntlets, and boots. The surcoat worn over the armor carries the kingdom's colors and device, making allegiance visible at a distance. Lords' horses wear caparisons in kingdom colors. Standard soldiers' horses wear working tack.

The surcoat pattern varies by kingdom. The heraldic device is painted on the heater shield face. On a sword the cross appears naturally in the crossguard geometry — the weapon is already a symbol by its shape. On devotional metalwork the sun cross appears as an equal-armed cross within a circle, with spoke-work between the arms and a gemstone at the center for consecrated pieces.

Red & Blue

Dravek

Red Eagle on Blue — quartered surcoat field

Active Conflict

Dravek is the home kingdom — the place from which most stories in this world begin. It sits in the central-western portion of the continent, sharing its longest border with Kelnvar to the east. The two kingdoms are close enough in culture and language that the argument about where one ends and the other begins has been running for three generations.

The eagle has been Dravek's device since before anyone currently living can remember choosing it. Red on blue. Aggressive posture, wings spread, talons displayed. It does not look like a kingdom that intends to lose ground, which makes the current situation in Strelnov a matter of some embarrassment to the crown.

Dravek is currently on the back foot in the Strelnov silver engagement. Eight to eleven months of back-and-forth fighting has left them holding less ground than they started with. The push to reclaim that ground is the immediate military priority — a priority that was complicated without warning by what came down from the Greymarch.

Kelnvar

Coiled Serpent on Yellow — whole body forms the device

Active Conflict

Kelnvar sits to the east of Dravek, sharing the long disputed border. The two kingdoms are close enough in language and custom to argue about it, and have been — occasionally with armies. The current war is the most active phase of that argument in living memory.

The coiled serpent is their device, styled so that the entire body of the creature forms the shape of the emblem — coiled tight, head raised. Pale yellow and green. The serpent has been their symbol longer than anyone remembers the reason for choosing it. Given what lives in the wilderness beyond the settlements, that lack of memory may be more interesting than it first appears.

Kelnvar is currently pressing the advantage in Strelnov. They hold more ground than they started with and are in the stronger position of the two kingdoms in the current engagement. Holding that ground against a Dravek counteroffensive is their immediate military concern.

The Five

Background Kingdoms

Five kingdoms exist beyond the immediate conflict. They are present in the world, present on the map, present in trade and diplomacy. They have all received the reports from the Greymarch.

Grey & White

Valdrek

Rampant Bear on Grey

Northern Kingdom

Northern kingdom. Grey forests, long winters, hard people. The bear stands rampant on grey — not subtle, not intended to be. Valdrek has been fighting its climate longer than it has been fighting its neighbors, and the two experiences have produced the same result.

Grimholt

Far Northern Kingdom

Further north than Valdrek, harder and leaner. The wolf on their field runs rather than stands — it is not posturing, it is moving. Black and silver. No warmth in those colors. The northernmost inhabited kingdom, and fully aware of what lies beyond them.

White & Deep Blue

Merath

Lion Passant on White — walking, one paw raised

Western Kingdom

Western kingdom, possibly coastal. The most politically sophisticated of the seven. The lion on their field walks rather than rages — calm, deliberate, watchful. Merath wins through patience and money. They have not lost a war in two generations, largely because they are careful about which wars they enter.

Red & Gold

Caldrath

Sun Disk with Rays — not an animal, a symbol

Southern Kingdom — Seat of the Faith

Southernmost and oldest. Caldrath has been here longer than the other kingdoms and is not shy about it. The Faith has its deepest roots here. The Chancellor's seat is in a city that technically belongs to no kingdom but sits within Caldrath's sphere of influence — which amounts to the same thing in practice.

Red and gold. The sun disk rather than an animal, because they have been here long enough to choose their own terms. Caldrath is watching the Greymarch incident with particular interest. The Faith's response will be shaped significantly by what comes out of the Chancellor's offices, and those offices are here.
